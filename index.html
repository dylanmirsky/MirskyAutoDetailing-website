Our Story

Built by Two Teenagers Who Genuinely Care

Dylan started Mirsky Auto Detailing at 14 years old — not with a shop or a large budget, but with a strong drive to do things right the first time. He began with interior detailing as his core focus, learning the craft properly before expanding. As his technique sharpened, so did his reputation. Word spread through the neighborhood fast.

By year two, the demand outgrew what one person could handle. Cormack joined as co-owner, bringing the same obsession with quality and a natural feel for the work. Together, they've built something real — a brand backed by genuine results, loyal customers, and a standard they hold themselves to on every single job.

This isn't a side hustle or a corporate franchise. It's our business, our name, and our reputation on the line every time we show up. That's exactly why we don't cut corners.

As a locally-owned operation without the overhead of a large shop, we're able to offer genuinely competitive pricing — often significantly less than what bigger detailers charge for the same quality of work. No hidden fees. No inflated quotes. Just honest detailing, fairly priced.

How long does a full detail take?+

A full detail is typically around 3 hours 45 minutes. An exterior-only takes about 45 minutes, and interior-only about 3 hours. Times vary based on vehicle size and condition — we'll always give you an honest estimate upfront.

Do you come to me, or do I drop off?+

Both! Drop your car at 4404 55th Ave NE, Seattle — no extra charge. Or we come to you for a flat $30 mobile fee. Same quality of work either way.

Why is there a fee for mobile service?+

Most detailers quietly fold a mobile fee into their base price — so you're paying it either way without knowing. We believe in being upfront: come to us, pay less. We come to you, the $30 covers travel time and logistics. You always know exactly where your money is going.

Why does the price vary?+

We quote based on your actual vehicle — not a flat rate that punishes owners of clean cars. Size, condition, pet hair, kids' messes, and heavy soiling all factor in. You never overpay for a car that doesn't need extra work.

How often should I get my car detailed?+

For most drivers, every 3 months is a solid cadence — but it really depends on how you use your car. Heavy daily drivers, families with kids, or pet owners may want every 2 months. Low-mileage or garage-kept vehicles can go 4–5 months comfortably. We'll recommend a schedule based on your situation.

Do you offer any guarantees?+

Yes. If you're not satisfied, reach out within 48 hours and we'll make it right at no additional charge. Our reputation depends on it.
